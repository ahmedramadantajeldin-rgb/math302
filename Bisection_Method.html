§ 01

Concept & Theory

Definition

The Bisection Method (Binary-Search Method) is a root-finding algorithm that repeatedly bisects an interval and selects the sub-interval in which the root must lie. It solves equations of the form \(f(x) = 0\), where \(f\) is a continuous function.

طريقة نصف المدى — تعمل بتقسيم الفترة باستمرار حتى الاقتراب من الجذر بدقة عالية.
Core Idea — Intermediate Value Theorem

Suppose \(f\) is continuous on \([a, b]\) and \(f(a) \cdot f(b) < 0\). By the Intermediate Value Theorem, there exists at least one \(P \in (a, b)\) such that \(f(P) = 0\).

The method exploits this by computing the midpoint \(x_n = \dfrac{a + b}{2}\) and deciding which half contains the root based on the sign of \(f(x_n)\). (يعني نتحقق من الإشارة في النقطة الوسطى)

Key Principle
Sign changes guarantee a root (IVT). No sign change → wrong interval.
Convergence Type
Linear — error halves each iteration. Reliable but slower than Newton.
بمعرفة أن الدالة تتغير إشارتها على الفترة، يوجد جذر بالضرورة — هذا هو أساس الطريقة.
Convergence Theorem

Suppose \(f \in C[a,b]\) and \(f(a)f(b) < 0\). The Bisection Method generates a sequence \(\{P_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}\) approximating a zero \(P\) of \(f\) with the bound:

\[ \boxed{|P_n - P| \;\leq\; \frac{b - a}{2^n}, \qquad n \geq 1} \]

As \(n \to \infty\), the error vanishes — convergence is guaranteed. (الخطأ يتقلص بنسبة النصف مع كل تكرار)

§ 02

Applicability Conditions

Required Conditions

Before applying the method on \([a,b]\), verify all three:

Condition 1 — Continuity
\(f\) must be continuous on \([a,b]\)
i.e. \(f \in C[a,b]\)
Condition 2 — Sign Change
\(f(a) \cdot f(b) < 0\) — opposite signs at endpoints
Condition 3 — Root Existence
At least one root must exist in the interior \((a, b)\)
Initial Midpoint
\(x_0 = \dfrac{a + b}{2}\) — always the first guess
الشرط الأساسي: f(a) و f(b) تختلفان في الإشارة (ضربهما سالب) — هذا يضمن وجود جذر.
§ 03

Algorithm Steps

Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Compute the midpoint at iteration \(n\):
\[ x_n \;=\; \frac{a_n + b_n}{2} \]
يعني نأخذ النقطة الوسطى بين a و b دائماً.
2
Evaluate \(f(x_n)\) and check termination: if \(f(x_n) = 0\) exactly, then \(P = x_n\). Stop.
3
Update the interval by the sign rule:
\[ \text{If } f(a_n)\cdot f(x_n) < 0 \;\Rightarrow\; \text{root in }[a_n,\,x_n] \quad\text{set}\quad b_{n+1} = x_n \] \[ \text{Else} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{root in }[x_n,\,b_n] \quad\text{set}\quad a_{n+1} = x_n \]
احتفظ بالفترة التي تتعارض إشارتا طرفيها.
4
Repeat steps 1–3 until \(\dfrac{b_n - a_n}{2} < \varepsilon\) (desired accuracy is met). نكرر حتى يصبح طول الفترة أصغر من الدقة المطلوبة.
Decision Rule — Quick Reference

Let \(s = \text{sign}(f(a_n))\). Then:

\[ \text{If } f(x_n) \times s < 0 \;\Rightarrow\; \text{root in left half} \qquad \text{else} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{root in right half} \]

Keep whichever sub-interval has endpoints with opposite signs.

§ 04

Convergence & Accuracy

Error Bound

After \(n\) iterations, the absolute error satisfies:

\[ |P_n - P| \;\leq\; \frac{b - a}{2^n} \]

The interval length halves with every step — guaranteed linear convergence.

الخطأ بعد n تكرار لا يتجاوز (b−a)/2ⁿ — يتقلص النصف في كل خطوة.
Relative Accuracy

The relative accuracy at step \(n\) is bounded by:

\[ \text{accuracy} \;\leq\; \frac{|x - x_n|}{|x_n|} \;\leq\; \frac{(b-a)/2^n}{|x_n|} \]

Where \(x_n\) is the midpoint at iteration \(n\). (الدقة النسبية = الخطأ المطلق ÷ القيمة)

Minimum Iterations for Desired Accuracy

To guarantee \(|P_n - P| < \varepsilon\), we need:

\[ \frac{b-a}{2^n} < \varepsilon \;\implies\; n \;>\; \frac{\ln(b-a) - \ln(\varepsilon)}{\ln 2} \;=\; \frac{\ln\!\left(\dfrac{b-a}{\varepsilon}\right)}{\ln 2} \]

For the common case \(\varepsilon = 10^{-k}\) with interval length \(b - a = 1\):

\[ n \;>\; \frac{k \ln 10}{\ln 2} \;\approx\; 3.3219 \cdot k \]

Always round up to the nearest integer. (نقرّب دائماً للأعلى)

Proof — Why Bisection Converges

At each step the interval halves. Starting with \([a_0,b_0]\) of length \(L_0 = b-a\):

After 1 iteration: length = L₀ / 2
After 2 iterations: length = L₀ / 4
After n iterations: length = L₀ / 2ⁿ = (b−a) / 2ⁿ

Since the true root \(P\) is always inside the current interval, the distance from any midpoint \(P_n\) to \(P\) cannot exceed half the current interval length:

\[ |P_n - P| \;\leq\; \frac{b-a}{2^n} \]

As \(n \to \infty\), \(\dfrac{b-a}{2^n} \to 0\), so \(P_n \to P\).  \(\blacksquare\)
