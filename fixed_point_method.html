§ 01

Theory & Method Explanation

Definition — Fixed Point

A number $a$ is called a fixed point of a function $g(x)$ if: $$g(a) = a$$ Geometrically, this means the graph of $y = g(x)$ intersects the line $y = x$ at $x = a$. النقطة الثابتة: حيث يساوي المخرج المدخل

Quick Examples
  • $f(x) = x^2$ → fixed points at $x=0$ and $x=1$, since $f(0)=0$ and $f(1)=1$
  • $g(x) = x^2 - 2$ → fixed point at $x=2$, since $g(2) = 4-2 = 2$ ✓
🌿 Root Finding
Find $x$ such that: $$f(x) = 0$$ i.e., where the curve crosses the x-axis
📌 Fixed Point
Find $x$ such that: $$g(x) = x$$ i.e., where the curve meets the diagonal $y=x$

The key insight: any root-finding problem $f(x)=0$ can be rewritten as a fixed point problem $x = g(x)$ by rearranging the equation algebraically. This is the bridge between the two formulations. إعادة صياغة المسألة

Steps

Algorithm — Step-by-Step

1
Rewrite as Fixed-Point Form Given $f(x)=0$, rearrange to get $x = g(x)$. There may be multiple valid rearrangements — choose the one for which convergence holds.
2
Verify Existence on $[a, b]$ Check that $g$ maps the interval into itself:
$$g(a) \in [a, b] \quad \text{and} \quad g(b) \in [a, b]$$
This guarantees a fixed point exists in the interval. إثبات وجود النقطة الثابتة
3
Check Convergence Criterion Compute $|g'(a)|$ and $|g'(b)|$ and take:
$$k = \max|g'(x)| \quad \text{over } [a,b]$$
If $k < 1$, the method converges. If $k \geq 1$ at any endpoint, adjust the interval.
4
Choose Initial Guess $x_0 \in [a, b]$ Any value in the interval works. Common choices are $x_0 = a$, $x_0 = b$, or the midpoint.
5
Iterate Apply the recurrence relation repeatedly:
$$x_n = g(x_{n-1}), \quad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$$
Continue until the desired number of iterations or until $|x_n - x_{n-1}|$ is small enough.
6
Compute Accuracy After $n$ Iterations
$$\text{Accuracy} \;\leq\; \frac{k}{1-k} \cdot \frac{|x_n - x_{n-1}|}{|x_n|}$$
Theorem

Convergence — Explanation

Theorem (Fixed Point Theorem): Let $g:[a,b]\to[a,b]$ be a continuous function. If $|g'(x)| \leq k < 1$ for all $x \in [a,b]$, then:

  1. There exists a unique fixed point $x^* \in [a,b]$.
  2. For any starting point $x_0 \in [a,b]$, the sequence $x_{n+1} = g(x_n)$ converges to $x^*$.
  3. The error satisfies: $|x_n - x^*| \leq \dfrac{k^n}{1-k}|x_1 - x_0|$.

Intuition الحدس الهندسي: when $|g'(x)| < 1$, the function $g$ is a contraction — it squeezes distances together. Each iteration brings $x_n$ closer to the fixed point. The smaller $k$ is, the faster the convergence.

Convergence Speed — Rule of Thumb
  • $k \approx 0.1$ → Very fast convergence (errors shrink 10× per step)
  • $k \approx 0.5$ → Moderate convergence (errors halve each step)
  • $k \approx 0.9$ → Slow convergence (errors shrink only 10% per step)
  • $k \geq 1$ → Divergence — method fails
Proof

Why The Method Works — Justification

Proof sketch (using the Mean Value Theorem):

Let $x^*$ be a fixed point, so $x^* = g(x^*)$. For any iterate $x_n$:

$$|x_{n+1} - x^*| = |g(x_n) - g(x^*)| = |g'(\xi)| \cdot |x_n - x^*|$$

where $\xi$ is some point between $x_n$ and $x^*$ (by MVT). Since $|g'(\xi)| \leq k < 1$:

$$|x_{n+1} - x^*| \leq k \cdot |x_n - x^*|$$

Applying this recursively:

$$|x_n - x^*| \leq k^n \cdot |x_0 - x^*| \xrightarrow{n\to\infty} 0$$

Since $k < 1$, we have $k^n \to 0$, so $x_n \to x^*$. The iteration converges. ✓

Solved Problems

01
Solved Problem

Polynomial Root — $x^3 - 2x^2 - 1 = 0$ on $[2, 3]$

Given: $g(x) = \dfrac{1}{x^2} + 2$.   (a) Show that $g$ has fixed points in $[2,3]$.   (b) Use fixed-point iteration to solve $x^3 - 2x^2 - 1 = 0$, performing 6 iterations.   (c) Find the accuracy after 6 iterations and check the convergence criterion.
A
Step A — Prove Existence of Fixed Point

We need to show that $g(a) \in [a,b]$ and $g(b) \in [a,b]$ for $[a,b] = [2,3]$:

$$g(2) = \frac{1}{(2)^2} + 2 = \frac{1}{4} + 2 = 2.25 \;\in\; [2, 3] \quad \checkmark$$
$$g(3) = \frac{1}{(3)^2} + 2 = \frac{1}{9} + 2 \approx 2.111 \;\in\; [2, 3] \quad \checkmark$$

∴ $g(x)$ maps $[2,3]$ into itself — a fixed point exists in $[2,3]$. ثبت وجود نقطة ثابتة

B
Step B — Convergence Criterion

Compute $g'(x)$:

$$g(x) = x^{-2} + 2 \;\Longrightarrow\; g'(x) = -\frac{2}{x^3}$$

Evaluate at endpoints:

$$|g'(2)| = \frac{2}{8} = 0.25 < 1 \quad \checkmark \qquad |g'(3)| = \frac{2}{27} \approx 0.074 < 1 \quad \checkmark$$
$$k = \max|g'(x)| \text{ on } [2,3] = 0.25 < 1$$

✓ Convergent   The method will converge since $k = 0.25 < 1$. Convergence is guaranteed. الطريقة متقاربة

C
Step C — Derive the Iteration Formula

Starting from $x^3 - 2x^2 - 1 = 0$:

$$x^3 = 2x^2 + 1 \;\Longrightarrow\; x \cdot x^2 = 2x^2 + 1 \;\Longrightarrow\; x = 2 + \frac{1}{x^2} = g(x)$$
$$\boxed{x_n = g(x_{n-1}) = 2 + \frac{1}{x_{n-1}^2}}$$

Starting value: $x_0 = 2$ (left endpoint of $[2,3]$). اختيار نقطة البداية

Step E — Accuracy After 6 Iterations
Accuracy Formula
$$\text{Accuracy} \;\leq\; \frac{k}{1-k} \cdot \frac{|x_6 - x_5|}{|x_6|}$$
$$|x_6 - x_5| = |2.205559679 - 2.205621742| = 6.206 \times 10^{-5}$$
$$\text{Accuracy} \leq \frac{0.25}{1 - 0.25} \cdot \frac{6.206 \times 10^{-5}}{2.205559679} = \frac{0.25}{0.75} \cdot 2.816 \times 10^{-5}$$

We have at least 5 correct decimal digits — excellent precision! (دقة عالية)

Root approximation: $x^* \approx 2.20556$ (6-iteration estimate)

02
Solved Problem

Transcendental Equation — $e^{-x} - x = 0$ on $[0,1]$ → adjusted to $[0.1, 1]$

Given: $g(x) = e^{-x}$.   (a) Show that $g$ has fixed points in $[0,1]$.   (b) Verify convergence criterion (with interval adjustment if needed).   (c) Perform 6 iterations and compute accuracy.
A
Step A — Existence on $[0,1]$
$$g(0) = e^0 = 1 \;\in\; [0, 1] \quad \checkmark \qquad g(1) = e^{-1} \approx 0.3679 \;\in\; [0, 1] \quad \checkmark$$

∴ $g(x)$ maps $[0,1]$ into itself — a fixed point exists in $[0,1]$.

B
Step B — Convergence Criterion on $[0,1]$
$$g'(x) = -e^{-x} \;\Longrightarrow\; |g'(x)| = e^{-x}$$
$$|g'(0)| = e^0 = 1 \quad \not{<}\; 1 \quad \text{⚠️ Fails!} \qquad |g'(1)| = e^{-1} \approx 0.368 < 1 \quad \checkmark$$
⚠️ Problem at $x = 0$

Since $|g'(0)| = 1 \not< 1$, the convergence condition is not satisfied at $x = 0$. We must adjust the interval to exclude $x = 0$. يجب تغيير المجال

New interval: $[0.1, 1]$

B₂
Step B₂ — Verify on Adjusted Interval $[0.1, 1]$

Existence check:

$$g(0.1) = e^{-0.1} = 0.9048 \;\in\; [0.1, 1] \quad \checkmark \qquad g(1) = e^{-1} = 0.3679 \;\in\; [0.1, 1] \quad \checkmark$$

Convergence check:

$$|g'(0.1)| = e^{-0.1} = 0.9048 < 1 \quad \checkmark \qquad |g'(1)| = e^{-1} = 0.3679 < 1 \quad \checkmark$$
$$k = \max|g'(x)| \text{ on } [0.1,1] = 0.9048$$

✓ Convergent   $k = 0.9048 < 1$ on the adjusted interval. Note: convergence will be slower than Problem 1 ($k$ is close to 1). تقارب بطيء

C
Step C — Iteration Formula
$$e^{-x} - x = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; x = e^{-x} = g(x)$$
$$\boxed{x_n = e^{-x_{n-1}}, \quad x_0 = 1}$$

Step E — Accuracy After 6 Iterations
$$|x_6 - x_5| = |0.579612335 - 0.545395786| = 0.034216549$$
$$\text{Accuracy} \leq \frac{0.9048}{1 - 0.9048} \cdot \frac{0.034216549}{0.579612335} = \frac{0.9048}{0.0952} \cdot 0.059032$$

Accuracy ≤ 0.5613
Only ~1 correct digit after 6 iterations — because $k \approx 0.9$ (slow convergence). More iterations are needed for good precision. (دقة منخفضة — يحتاج تكرارات أكثر)
💡 Comparison of the Two Problems

Problem 1 ($k=0.25$) achieves accuracy $\approx 10^{-6}$ in 6 steps. Problem 2 ($k=0.9$) achieves only $\approx 0.56$ accuracy in 6 steps. This illustrates why a smaller $k$ means dramatically faster convergence. مقارنة معدل التقارب
