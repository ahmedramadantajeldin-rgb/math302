§ 01

Definitions & Theory

Differential Equation — Core Concepts

Differential Equation (D.E.): An equation relating an unknown function \( y(x) \) to one or more of its derivatives. The solution is finding the function \( y(x) \) that satisfies the equation — analytically or numerically.

Order of D.E.

The highest derivative present in the equation.

\( y'' + 2y' = 0 \) → Order = 2

Degree of D.E.

The power of the highest derivative.

\( (y'')^3 + y = 0 \) → Degree = 3

§ 02

Euler Method — Algorithm

Core Idea & Intuition

طريقة Euler هي أبسط طريقة عددية لحل الـ ODE من الدرجة الأولى.

الفكرة الأساسية: بدل ما نحل المعادلة تحليلياً، إحنا بنقرّب الحل بتدريجياً — خطوة خطوة — من نقطة معروفة لنقطة مجهولة، باستخدام المشتقة كميل تقريبي.

يعني لو عندي القيمة \( y_0 \) عند \( x_0 \)، وعارف الميل \( y'_0 \) عند تلك النقطة من المعادلة التفاضلية، أقدر أحسب قيمة \( y \) عند الخطوة التالية \( x_1 = x_0 + h \) مباشرة.

Recurrence Formula
\[ y_{n+1} = y_n + h \cdot y'_n \]

where \( h \) is the step size, and \( y'_n = f(x_n,\, y_n) \) is evaluated directly from the given ODE at each row.

Step Size:

\[ h = \frac{X - x_0}{n} \]

where \( X \) is the target x-value and \( n \) is the number of steps.

Error Formula — Local Truncation Error

الخطأ في طريقة Euler بيجي من أننا بنستخدم الميل عند بداية الخطوة فقط، مش متوسط الميل على طولها. ده بيعمل خطأ محلي لكل خطوة يتحسب من:

\[ \text{Error} \leq \frac{1}{2}\, h^2\, y'' \]

\( y'' \) is the second derivative of \( y \) (obtained by differentiating the ODE), evaluated at the final computed point.

Solution Procedure — Step by Step

خطوات الحل دايماً ثابتة. اتبعها بالترتيب ده بدون ما تتخطى خطوة:

1
Identify the given data: Extract \( x_0 \), \( y_0 \), target value \( X \), and either \( h \) or \( n \). لو معطي \( n \) بدل \( h \): احسب \( h = (X - x_0)/n \) أولاً قبل ما تبدأ الجدول.
2
Build the iteration table: Columns are \( x_n \), \( y_n \), and \( h \cdot y'_n \). في كل صف: احسب \( y'_n = f(x_n, y_n) \) بتعويض القيم مباشرة في المعادلة التفاضلية، واضرب في \( h \).
3
Advance the solution: \( y_{n+1} = y_n + h \cdot y'_n \) — add column 3 to column 2 to get the next row. كرّر لحد ما تعدي عند قيمة \( x = X \) المطلوبة.
4
Compute error (if required): Differentiate the ODE to get \( y'' \), substitute final \( (x, y) \) values, then apply \( \text{Error} = \frac{1}{2} h^2 y'' \).
§ 03

Example 1

01
Euler Method · Step Size Given · Error Required
Use an approximate method to find \( y(0.8) \) by iteration, given \( \dfrac{dy}{dx} = x + y^3 \) with \( y(0) = 0 \) and step size \( h = 0.2 \). Find the error.
Given Data
\( \dfrac{dy}{dx} = x + y^3 \)
\( x_0 = 0,\quad y_0 = 0 \)
\( h = 0.2 \)
Find \( y(0.8) \) + Error
Iteration Table

Applying \( y_{n+1} = y_n + h\,y'_n \) with \( h\,y'_n = 0.2\,(x_n + y_n^3) \):

\( x_n \) \( y_n \) \( h \cdot y'_n \;=\; 0.2\,(x_n + y_n^3) \)
0 0 \( 0.2\,(0 + 0^3) = 0 \)
0.2 0 \( 0.2\,(0.2 + 0^3) = 0.04 \)
0.4 0.04 \( 0.2\,(0.4 + (0.04)^3) = 0.0800128 \)
0.6 0.1200128 \( 0.2\,(0.6 + (0.1200128)^3) = 0.1203457106 \)
0.8 0.2403585106 —
How each y is computed:
\( y_1 = 0 + 0 = 0 \quad\to\quad y_2 = 0 + 0.04 = 0.04 \quad\to\quad y_3 = 0.04 + 0.0800128 = 0.1200128 \quad\to\quad y_4 = 0.1200128 + 0.1203457106 = 0.2403585106 \)
✓
Final Answer — y(0.8)
y(0.8) ≈ 0.2403585
Obtained after 4 Euler iterations with h = 0.2
Error Calculation

Step 1 — Differentiate the ODE to obtain \( y'' \):

\[ y' = x + y^3 \] \[ y'' = \frac{d}{dx}\bigl(x + y^3\bigr) = 1 + 3y^2 \cdot y' = 1 + 3y^2\,(x + y^3) \]

Step 2 — Evaluate at the final values \( x = 0.8 \), \( y = 0.2403585106 \):

\[ y'' = 1 + 3\,(0.2403585106)^2\,(0.8 + (0.2403585106)^3) \] \[ y'' = 1 + 3\,(0.057772)\,(0.8 + 0.013878) \] \[ y'' = 1 + 3\,(0.057772)\,(0.813878) \approx 1.141059995 \]

Step 3 — Apply the local truncation error formula:

\[ \text{Error} = \frac{1}{2}\,h^2\,y'' = \frac{1}{2}\,(0.2)^2\,(1.141059995) = \frac{1}{2}\,(0.04)\,(1.141059995) \]
⚠
Local Truncation Error
Error ≤ 0.0228211999

Example 2

02
Euler Method · Number of Steps Given
Use a numerical method to calculate \( y(0.3) \) by iteration, given \( \dfrac{dy}{dx} = 2y^2 + x^3 \) with \( y(0) = 1 \). Use \( n = 3 \) steps.
Given Data
\( y' = 2y^2 + x^3 \)
\( x_0 = 0,\quad y_0 = 1 \)
\( n = 3,\quad X = 0.3 \)
Step Size Calculation
\[ h = \frac{X - x_0}{n} = \frac{0.3 - 0}{3} = 0.1 \]
Iteration Table

Applying \( y_{n+1} = y_n + h\,y'_n \) with \( h\,y'_n = 0.1\,(2y_n^2 + x_n^3) \):

\( x_n \) \( y_n \) \( h \cdot y'_n \;=\; 0.1\,(2y_n^2 + x_n^3) \)
0 1 \( 0.1\,(2(1)^2 + 0^3) = 0.2 \)
0.1 1.2 \( 0.1\,(2(1.2)^2 + (0.1)^3) = 0.2881 \)
0.2 1.4881 \( 0.1\,(2(1.4881)^2 + (0.2)^3) = 0.443688322 \)
0.3 1.931788322 —
Verification of row 2 computation:
\( 2(1.2)^2 = 2(1.44) = 2.88 \quad;\quad (0.1)^3 = 0.001 \quad;\quad h\,y'_1 = 0.1\times 2.881 = 0.2881 \)
\( y_2 = 1.2 + 0.2881 = 1.4881 \;\checkmark \)
✓
Final Answer — y(0.3)
y(0.3) ≈ 1.931788322
Obtained after 3 Euler iterations with h = 0.1

Example 3

03
Euler Method · 4 Iterations · Error Required
Use a numerical method to solve \( y' = x^2 + y^2 \) and find \( y(1) \), given \( y(0) = 1 \), using 4 iterations. Find the error.
Given Data
\( y' = x^2 + y^2 \)
\( x_0 = 0,\quad y_0 = 1 \)
\( n = 4,\quad X = 1 \)
Find \( y(1) \) + Error
Step Size Calculation
\[ h = \frac{X - x_0}{n} = \frac{1 - 0}{4} = 0.25 \]
Iteration Table

Applying \( y_{n+1} = y_n + h\,y'_n \) with \( h\,y'_n = 0.25\,(x_n^2 + y_n^2) \):

\( x_n \) \( y_n \) \( h \cdot y'_n \;=\; 0.25\,(x_n^2 + y_n^2) \)
0 1 \( 0.25\,(0^2 + 1^2) = 0.25 \)
0.25 1.25 \( 0.25\,(0.25^2 + 1.25^2) = 0.40625 \)
0.50 1.65625 \( 0.25\,(0.5^2 + 1.65625^2) \approx 0.74829 \)
0.75 2.40454 \( 0.25\,(0.75^2 + 2.40454^2) \approx 1.58598 \)
1.00 3.99052 —
Verification of row 3 computation:
\( 0.5^2 = 0.25 \;;\quad 1.65625^2 \approx 2.7432 \;;\quad h\,y'_2 = 0.25\times(0.25 + 2.7432) = 0.25\times 2.9932 = 0.74829 \)
\( y_3 = 1.25 + 0.40625 = 1.65625 \;\to\; y_4 = 1.65625 + 0.74829 = 2.40454 \;\checkmark \)
✓
Final Answer — y(1)
y(1) ≈ 3.99052
Obtained after 4 Euler iterations with h = 0.25
Error Calculation

Step 1 — Differentiate the ODE to obtain \( y'' \):

\[ y' = x^2 + y^2 \] \[ y'' = \frac{d}{dx}\bigl(x^2 + y^2\bigr) = 2x + 2y \cdot y' = 2x + 2y\,(x^2 + y^2) \]

Step 2 — Evaluate at the final values \( x = 1 \), \( y = 3.99052 \):

\[ y'' = 2(1) + 2(3.99052)\bigl((1)^2 + (3.99052)^2\bigr) \] \[ y'' = 2 + 7.98104 \times (1 + 15.9242) \] \[ y'' = 2 + 7.98104 \times 16.9242 \approx 137.0829 \]

Step 3 — Apply the local truncation error formula:

\[ \text{Error} = \frac{1}{2}\,h^2\,y'' = \frac{1}{2}\,(0.25)^2\,(137.0829) = \frac{1}{2}\,(0.0625)\,(137.0829) \]
⚠
Local Truncation Error
Error ≤ 4.28384
