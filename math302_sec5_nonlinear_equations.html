Math 302 — Section 5

System of Non-Linear Equations

Fixed-Point Iteration Method

📖 Iterative Scheme 📊 Convergence Test 🔢 Accuracy Formula 📝 2 Solved Examples

1Introduction

📌 What is the Fixed-Point (Jacobi-style) Iteration for Non-linear Systems?

When we have a system of two non-linear equations in two unknowns $x$ and $y$, we cannot simply use Gaussian elimination. Instead, we rearrange the equations to express $x$ as a function of $y$, and $y$ as a function of $x$ — then iterate (repeat) until the values converge.

This approach is analogous to the Jacobi iteration used for linear systems, now extended to handle non-linearity.

Linear vs. Non-linear Systems

🔵 Linear System

  • All unknowns appear to the power of 1
  • e.g., $2x + 3y = 7$
  • Can be solved with Gaussian elimination, Gauss-Seidel, etc.
  • Unique solution (if non-singular)

🟣 Non-linear System

  • Unknowns appear as powers, products, or functions
  • e.g., $y = x^4$, $x^3 + y^3 = 8$
  • Requires iterative numerical methods
  • May have multiple solutions
✅ Why use this method? It is simple to implement, requires only basic algebra to rearrange the equations, and converges reliably when the spectral condition $\|G_0\| < 1$ is satisfied.

2Method Steps

Given a system of two non-linear equations in $x$ and $y$, follow these four steps:

1
Rearrange the equations إعادة ترتيب المعادلات
From one equation, isolate $x$ in terms of $y$ → call this function $g_1(y)$.
From the other equation, isolate $y$ in terms of $x$ → call this $g_2(x)$.
$x = g_1(y) \qquad y = g_2(x)$
2
Build the Jacobian matrix $G$ مصفوفة المشتقات
Compute the matrix of partial derivatives of $(g_1, g_2)$ with respect to $(x, y)$:
$$G = \begin{pmatrix} \dfrac{\partial g_1}{\partial x} & \dfrac{\partial g_2}{\partial x} \\[10pt] \dfrac{\partial g_1}{\partial y} & \dfrac{\partial g_2}{\partial y} \end{pmatrix}$$
Then evaluate at the starting point $(x_0, y_0)$ to get $G_0 = G\big|_{(x_0, y_0)}$.
3
Check convergence شرط التقارب
Compute the norm of $G_0$ (maximum absolute entry):
$$\|G_0\| = \max_{i,j}\,|g_{ij}|$$
If $\|G_0\| < 1$ → The method is Convergent ✅
If $\|G_0\| \geq 1$ → The method Diverges ❌
4
Iterate and build the table التكرار وبناء الجدول
Starting from $(x_0, y_0)$, apply:
$x_{n} = g_1(y_{n-1}), \qquad y_{n} = g_2(x_{n-1})$
Fill in an iteration table: $n \;|\; x_n \;|\; y_n$ until convergence or the required number of iterations.
5
Calculate accuracy (error bound) حساب الدقة
After $n$ iterations, estimate the accuracy using:
$$\text{accuracy} \;\leq\; \frac{\|G_0\|}{1 - \|G_0\|} \cdot \frac{\|\mathbf{x}^{(n)} - \mathbf{x}^{(n-1)}\|}{\|\mathbf{x}^{(n)}\|}$$
where $\|\mathbf{x}^{(n)}\| = \max\bigl(|x_n|,\,|y_n|\bigr)$ and $\|\mathbf{x}^{(n)} - \mathbf{x}^{(n-1)}\| = \max\bigl(|x_n - x_{n-1}|,\,|y_n - y_{n-1}|\bigr)$.

3Key Formulas Reference

📋 Quick Reference — All You Need

Iterative Scheme
$x_n = g_1(y_{n-1})$
$y_n = g_2(x_{n-1})$
Convergence Condition
$\|G_0\| < 1 \;\Rightarrow\;$ Convergent
$$G = \begin{pmatrix} \dfrac{\partial g_1}{\partial x} & \dfrac{\partial g_2}{\partial x} \\[12pt] \dfrac{\partial g_1}{\partial y} & \dfrac{\partial g_2}{\partial y} \end{pmatrix} \Bigg|_{(x_0,\,y_0)}$$
$$\text{Accuracy} \;\leq\; \frac{\|G_0\|}{1 - \|G_0\|} \cdot \frac{\left\|\mathbf{x}^{(n)} - \mathbf{x}^{(n-1)}\right\|}{\left\|\mathbf{x}^{(n)}\right\|}$$
📌 Norm used: The norm $\|\cdot\|$ is the max-norm (∞-norm): for a vector $\mathbf{v} = (a,b)$: $\|\mathbf{v}\| = \max(|a|,|b|)$. For a matrix: $\|G\| = \max$ of all absolute entry values.

4Example 1 — Solved

Example 1

Solve using an iterative scheme — convergence check + 6 iterations + accuracy

Problem Statement
Solve the system of equations using an iterative scheme: $$y = x^4, \qquad x^3 + y^3 = 8$$ Starting point: $x_0 = 1,\; y_0 = 1$.
State why the method is convergent. Find the accuracy after 6 iterations.
Step 1 — Rearrange Equations إعادة الترتيب

We need to express $x$ from one equation and $y$ from the other.

From equation 1: $y = x^4$

Isolate $x$ in terms of $y$:

$$x^4 = y \;\Rightarrow\; x = y^{1/4}$$
$g_1: \quad x = y^{1/4}$

We take the positive root since $x_0 = 1 > 0$.

From equation 2: $x^3 + y^3 = 8$

Isolate $y$ in terms of $x$:

$$y^3 = 8 - x^3 \;\Rightarrow\; y = \sqrt[3]{8 - x^3}$$
$g_2: \quad y = (8 - x^3)^{1/3}$
Step 2 — Build the Jacobian Matrix $G$ مصفوفة G

We have $g_1 = y^{1/4}$ (function of $y$ only) and $g_2 = (8-x^3)^{1/3}$ (function of $x$ only). Compute all partial derivatives:

$\dfrac{\partial g_1}{\partial x} = 0$
($g_1$ has no $x$)
$\dfrac{\partial g_2}{\partial x} = \dfrac{1}{3}(8-x^3)^{-2/3}\cdot(-3x^2)$
$= -x^2(8-x^3)^{-2/3}$
$\dfrac{\partial g_1}{\partial y} = \dfrac{1}{4}y^{-3/4}$
$\dfrac{\partial g_2}{\partial y} = 0$
($g_2$ has no $y$)
$$G = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -x^2(8-x^3)^{-2/3} \\[8pt] \dfrac{1}{4}y^{-3/4} & 0 \end{pmatrix}$$
Step 3 — Evaluate $G_0$ at $(x_0, y_0) = (1,1)$ تعويض النقطة الابتدائية

Substitute $x=1$, $y=1$ into every entry of $G$:

$G_{11} = 0$
$G_{12} = -(1)^2 \cdot (8-1)^{-2/3} = -(7)^{-2/3} = -0.2732758833$
$G_{21} = \dfrac{1}{4}(1)^{-3/4} = \dfrac{1}{4} = 0.25$
$G_{22} = 0$
$$G_0 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -0.2732758833 \\[6pt] \dfrac{1}{4} & 0 \end{pmatrix}$$
$$\|G_0\| = \max\bigl(0,\; 0.2732758833,\; 0.25,\; 0\bigr) = 0.2732758833$$
Since $\|G_0\| = 0.2732758833 < 1$ → ✅ The method is Convergent
Step 4 — Manual First Iteration (n=1) التكرار الأول يدوياً
$$x_1 = g_1(y_0) = (1)^{1/4} = 1$$
$$y_1 = g_2(x_0) = (8 - 1^3)^{1/3} = 7^{1/3} \approx 1.912931183$$
Step 5 — Full Iteration Table (6 Iterations) جدول التكرار

Applying $x_n = y_{n-1}^{1/4}$ and $y_n = (8 - x_{n-1}^3)^{1/3}$ repeatedly:

$n$ $x_n$ $y_n$
0 $x_0 = 1$ $y_0 = 1$
1 1 1.912931183
2 1.176047429 1.854062020
3 1.166893308 1.857709472
4 1.167466557 1.857483066
5 1.167430985 1.857497118
6 1.167433193 1.857496246
$\mathbf{x}^{(6)}-\mathbf{x}^{(5)}$ $2.208 \times 10^{-6}$ $-8.72 \times 10^{-7}$
🔍 Norm calculations from table:
$\|\mathbf{x}^{(6)} - \mathbf{x}^{(5)}\| = \max(2.208\times10^{-6},\; 8.72\times10^{-7}) = 2.208\times10^{-6}$
$\|\mathbf{x}^{(6)}\| = \max(|1.167433193|,\; |1.857496246|) = 1.857496246$
Step 6 — Calculate Accuracy حساب الدقة
Accuracy Formula Applied
$$\text{accuracy} \leq \frac{\|G_0\|}{1-\|G_0\|} \cdot \frac{\|\mathbf{x}^{(6)} - \mathbf{x}^{(5)}\|}{\|\mathbf{x}^{(6)}\|}$$
$$= \frac{0.2732758833}{1 - 0.2732758833} \cdot \frac{2.208\times10^{-6}}{1.857496246}$$
$$= \frac{0.2732758833}{0.7267241167} \cdot \frac{2.208\times10^{-6}}{1.857496246}$$
≤ 4.469951388 × 10⁻⁷

5Example 2 — Solved

Example 2

Solve two non-linear equations — convergence check + 7 iterations + accuracy

Problem Statement
Solve the system of non-linear equations: $$x - 2 + y^2 = 0, \qquad y = x^3$$ Starting point: $x_0 = -1,\; y_0 = -1$.
State why the method is convergent and calculate the accuracy after 7 iterations.
Step 1 — Rearrange Equations إعادة الترتيب
From equation 2: $y = x^3$

Isolate $x$ in terms of $y$:

$$x = y^{1/3} = \sqrt[3]{y}$$
$g_1: \quad x = y^{1/3}$
From equation 1: $x - 2 + y^2 = 0$

Isolate $y$ in terms of $x$:

$$y^2 = 2 - x \;\Rightarrow\; y = \pm\sqrt{2-x}$$
$g_2: \quad y = -\sqrt{2-x}$

We choose the negative root because $y_0 = -1 < 0$.

Step 2 — Build the Jacobian Matrix $G$ مصفوفة G
$\dfrac{\partial g_1}{\partial x} = 0$
($g_1 = y^{1/3}$ has no $x$)
$\dfrac{\partial g_2}{\partial x} = -\dfrac{1}{2}(2-x)^{-1/2}\cdot(-1) = \dfrac{1}{2}(2-x)^{-1/2}$
$\dfrac{\partial g_1}{\partial y} = \dfrac{1}{3}y^{-2/3}$
$\dfrac{\partial g_2}{\partial y} = 0$
($g_2 = -\sqrt{2-x}$ has no $y$)
$$G = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & \dfrac{1}{2}(2-x)^{-1/2} \\[10pt] \dfrac{1}{3}y^{-2/3} & 0 \end{pmatrix}$$
Step 3 — Evaluate $G_0$ at $(x_0, y_0) = (-1, -1)$ تعويض النقطة الابتدائية

Substitute $x = -1$, $y = -1$:

$G_{11} = 0$
$G_{12} = \dfrac{1}{2}(2-(-1))^{-1/2} = \dfrac{1}{2}(3)^{-1/2} = \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{3}} \approx 0.2886751346$
$G_{21} = \dfrac{1}{3}(-1)^{-2/3} = \dfrac{1}{3}(1) = \dfrac{1}{3} \approx 0.3333...$
$G_{22} = 0$
$$G_0 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0.2886751346 \\[6pt] \dfrac{1}{3} & 0 \end{pmatrix}$$
$$\|G_0\| = \max\!\left(0,\; 0.2886751346,\; \tfrac{1}{3},\; 0\right) = \max\!\left(0.2886751346,\; 0.3333...\right) = \frac{1}{3}$$
Since $\|G_0\| = \dfrac{1}{3} < 1$ → ✅ The method is Convergent
Step 4 — Full Iteration Table (7 Iterations) جدول التكرار

Applying $x_n = y_{n-1}^{1/3}$ and $y_n = -\sqrt{2 - x_{n-1}}$ repeatedly:

$n$ $x_n$ $y_n$
0 $x_0 = -1$ $y_0 = -1$
1 −1 −1.732050808
2 −1.200936955 −1.789116?5
3 −1.213983694 −1.792758683
4 −1.214806979 −1.792988282
5 −1.214858837 −1.793002743
6 −1.214862103 −1.793003654
7 −1.214862309 −1.793003711
$\mathbf{x}^{(7)}-\mathbf{x}^{(6)}$ $-2.06 \times 10^{-7}$ $-5.7 \times 10^{-8}$
🔍 Norm calculations from table:
$\|\mathbf{x}^{(7)} - \mathbf{x}^{(6)}\| = \max(2.06\times10^{-7},\; 5.7\times10^{-8}) = 2.06\times10^{-7}$
$\|\mathbf{x}^{(7)}\| = \max(|-1.214862309|,\; |-1.793003711|) = 1.793003711$
Step 5 — Calculate Accuracy حساب الدقة
Accuracy Formula Applied
$$\text{accuracy} \leq \frac{\|G_0\|}{1-\|G_0\|} \cdot \frac{\|\mathbf{x}^{(7)} - \mathbf{x}^{(6)}\|}{\|\mathbf{x}^{(7)}\|}$$
$$= \frac{1/3}{1 - 1/3} \cdot \frac{2.06\times10^{-7}}{1.793003711}$$
$$= \frac{1/3}{2/3} \cdot \frac{2.06\times10^{-7}}{1.793003711} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2.06\times10^{-7}}{1.793003711}$$
≤ 5.744550297 × 10⁻⁸

6Important Notes & Observations

📌 Choice of Sign

When isolating a variable involving $\pm$, always choose the sign that matches your starting point. In Example 2, since $y_0 = -1 < 0$, we chose $y = -\sqrt{2-x}$. Choosing the wrong sign would cause divergence.

🔢 Norm Definition

The norm used throughout is the max-norm (∞-norm). For a 2D vector $(a, b)$: $\|(a,b)\| = \max(|a|,|b|)$. For a 2×2 matrix: $\|G\| = $ maximum absolute entry.

⚠️ Convergence is Not Guaranteed

The convergence condition $\|G_0\| < 1$ is sufficient but not always necessary. The Jacobian matrix $G$ is evaluated at the starting point $(x_0, y_0)$ only. If $\|G_0\| \geq 1$, try a different rearrangement or a different starting point.

🎯 Accuracy vs. Absolute Error

The accuracy formula gives an upper bound on the relative error. A smaller result means higher confidence in the answer. The smaller $\|G_0\|$ is compared to 1, the faster the convergence and the smaller the accuracy bound.
